# Locations Gazetteer: The New World

## Overview
Fifty key locations spanning coasts, wilds, cities, and the Tomb's orbit. Each entry includes lore, exploration features, encounters, secrets, and campaign ties.

---

## 1. Oakhaven & The Salt Stair
*The Village & The Ruin*
**Region**: Western Coastline
**Faction Influence**: Oakhaven (Independent), Salt Stair (Nazarick/Goblins)

### Lore
**Oakhaven**: A peaceful timber village on the edge of the Whispering Woods. Known for its sturdy lumber and "The Golden Grain" bakery. Recently plagued by goblin raids.
**The Salt Stair**: A spiraling stone wharf jutting out of the ocean cliffs miles away. Ancient, haunted, and shrouded in brine fog. The goblins are dragging their captives here for some dark purpose.

### Exploration
- **Oakhaven Village**:
    - **Points of Interest**: Village Square, The Rusty Flagon (Tavern), Mayor's House, Blacksmith.
    - **Rumors**: "The Red-Eyed Ghost" leads the goblins.
- **The Whispering Woods**: Dense forest between the village and the coast. Warped by weak magic.
- **The Salt Stair (Ruins)**:
    - **Hazard**: Rising Tides flood the lower levels.
    - **Secret**: A Nazarick "Audit Cache" is hidden in the submerged chambers.

### Encounters (CR 1-3)
- **Goblin Raiding Party** (CR 1): 4 Goblins led by a Worg rider.
- **Red Eye's Lieutenant** (CR 2): A Hobgoblin commanding the forward camp.
- **Brine Wraiths** (CR 3): Guardians of the Salt Stair ruins.

### Campaign Ties
- **Session 1 Start**: The party gathers in Oakhaven to answer the Mayor's call.
- **The Hook**: Rescuing the blacksmith's daughter leads to a note pointing to the Salt Stair.
- **First Dungeon**: The Goblin Cave (in the woods) -> The Salt Stair (Session 3+).

## 2. Ashen Lyceum  
*Fallen Academy*  
**Region**: Central Plains  
**Faction Influence**: Choir (Sabotage), Independent Scholars

### Lore
Once a prestigious academy, now a blackened ruin after a catastrophic planar miscast. The surviving clockwork librarians continue their endless task, reshelving books that scream when touched.

### Features
- **Chronomantic Lectern**: Replays the final moments before the disaster in a 30-foot radius.
- **Null-Ink Codex**: Required to access the restricted section; reveals hidden text under specific conditions.
- **Hazard**: Wild magic zones (roll on the Wild Magic Surge table when casting spells).

### Encounters (CR 2-4)
- **Animated Curricula** (CR 2): Living spells that test intruders with deadly pop quizzes.
- **Dean's Echo** (CR 3): A spectral figure demanding ethical proofs to pass.
- **Choir Saboteurs** (CR 4): Attempting to erase evidence of their order's involvement.

### Secrets
- **Sabotage Node**: Hidden Choir device disrupting the local Weave.
- **Lesson Plans**: Demiurge's notes on "stress-testing" mortal institutions.

### Campaign Ties
- **Session 8**: Primary dungeon location.
- **Lyrin's Past**: Contains archives that may reveal his true nature.
- **Lore Drop**: Hints about the Great Cataclysm.

## 3. The Broken Crown Road  
*Campaign Spine*  
**Region**: Continent-spanning  
**Faction Influence**: Nazarick (Primary), All Major Factions

### Lore
A ritual highway constructed by Nazarick to measure the continent for the Grand Lattice. The road hums with barely-contained energy, its mileposts marking zones of controlled reality.

### Features
- **Audit Mileposts**: Checkpoints where reality is slightly... thinner.
- **Confiscation Courts**: Mobile tribunals that judge the worth of travelers' possessions.
- **Contraband Caches**: Hidden compartments used by the resistance.

### Encounters (Scaling CR)
- **Audit Teams** (CR varies): Nazarick officials conducting "routine inspections."
- **Refugee Caravans**: Desperate groups seeking safe passage.
- **Cartel Toll Collectors**: "Taxing" travelers under dubious authority.

### Secrets
- **Quiet Mile**: A hidden section where divination fails completely.
- **Lattice Conduit**: Access point to the Grand Lattice's control systems.

### Campaign Ties
- **Travel Backbone**: Connects major locations.
- **Faction Playground**: All major factions vie for control.
- **Clock Ticking**: The road's completion advances the doomsday clock.

## 4. The Ember Market  
*City Underside*  
**Region**: Free City of Lestria  
**Faction Influence**: Syndicate (Primary), Independent Operators

### Lore
A night bazaar thriving in the ruins of a burned-out amphitheater. The Market's golden rule: "No violence under the ward-stones." Break it, and you become part of the merchandise.

### Features
- **Anti-Scrying Wards**: No magical surveillance functions within the market.
- **Sigilware Stalls**: Black market magic items with... interesting histories.
- **The Crucible**: Arena where disputes are settled in blood or barter.

### Encounters (CR 2-5)
- **Broker Soot** (CR 3): Information merchant with a price for every secret.
- **Memory-Scribes** (CR 2): Willing to buy/sell/trade memories.
- **Choir Informants** (CR 4): Blending in, watching for heresy.

### Secrets
- **Nazarick Dropbox**: Hidden message cache behind a loose brick on the old stage.
- **The Silent Auction**: Where souls and secrets change hands.

### Campaign Ties
- **Gear Hub**: Source of specialized equipment.
- **Rumor Mill**: Learn about upcoming events.
- **Recruitment Ground**: Factions scout for talent here.

## 5. The Drowned Bell  
*Shrine on a Shoal*  
**Region**: Eastern Archipelago  
**Faction Influence**: Independent, Minor Cults

### Lore
A bronze bell that tolls beneath the waves during storms, calling the faithful and the damned. The shrine above the waterline is merely the tip of a much larger submerged structure.

### Features
- **Underwater Chambers**: Accessible during low tide or with water breathing.
- **Mirror Inscriptions**: Show different texts based on the viewer's sins.
- **Tidal Schedule**: The bell's magic waxes and wanes with the moon.

### Encounters (CR 1-4)
- **Salt Monks** (CR 1/2): Devotees who have adapted to amphibious life.
- **Brine Revenant** (CR 4): A paladin seeking absolution for past failures.
- **Drowned Ones** (CR 2): Those who answered the bell's call... permanently.

### Secrets
- **Parley Code**: Sequence of tones that summons a Nazarick courier.
- **Sunken Vault**: Contains relics from before the Founding.

### Campaign Ties
- **Moral Dilemma**: Help the revenant or claim his power?
- **Undead Ally**: Potential companion for coastal adventures.
- **Lore Drop**: Hints about the world before Nazarick.

## 6. The Sable Depot  
*Audit Waystation*  
**Region**: Northern Trade Routes  
**Faction Influence**: Nazarick (Primary)

### Lore
A spartan waystation where "aid" is dispensed with meticulous records and binding contracts. The air smells of ink and iron, and the walls are lined with ledger-golems recording every transaction.

### Features
- **Ledger-Golems**: Animated constructs that record compliance.
- **Policy Archives**: Endless shelves of regulations and precedents.
- **The Scales**: Magical device that weighs need against ability to pay.

### Encounters (CR 2-5)
- **Audit Clerk** (CR 1/4): Bureaucrats with surprising authority.
- **Compliance Enforcers** (CR 3): Heavily armored and utterly humorless.
- **Desperate Refugees** (CR 0): Willing to trade anything for supplies.

### Secrets
- **Handler Cache**: Lyrin's personal drop point.
- **Too Helpful Clerk**: Knows more than they should about the party.

### Campaign Ties
- **Supply Point**: But at what cost?
- **Demiurge's Web**: See the audit system in action.
- **Hard Choices**: Help refugees or maintain cover?

## 7. The Farsung Menhir  
*Ritual Beacon*  
**Region**: Western Highlands  
**Faction Influence**: Choir (Primary), Nazarick (Secondary)

### Lore
A towering standing stone that resonates with the harmonic frequencies of the Lattice. At dusk, it emits a tone that can be heard for miles, tuning the local Weave to Nazarick's specifications.

### Features
- **Harmonic Interface**: Skill challenge to adjust the tone.
- **Resonance Chambers**: Hidden caves where the stone's song is amplified.
- **Wild Magic Zones**: Unstable areas where magic behaves unpredictably.

### Encounters (CR 3-5)
- **Choir Unbinders** (CR 3): Attempting to disrupt the harmony.
- **Tuning Crew** (CR 4): Nazarick technicians maintaining the beacon.
- **Resonance Phantoms** (CR 2): Echoes of those absorbed by the stone.

### Secrets
- **Failed Experiments**: Pit of mutated creatures beneath the stone.
- **Tuning Fork**: Key item that can alter the Menhir's frequency.

### Campaign Ties
- **Faction Crossroads**: Help the Choir or Nazarick?
- **Lore Drop**: Nature of the Lattice revealed.
- **Environmental Hazard**: The wrong note could be catastrophic.

## 8. The Weeping Forge  
*War Foundry*  
**Region**: Southern Wastelands  
**Faction Influence**: Syndicate (Primary), Nazarick (Influence)

### Lore
A massive foundry producing "merciful" weapons designed to subdue rather than kill. The air is thick with the scent of molten metal and the constant sound of hammers on steel.

### Features
- **Heat Zones**: Areas of extreme temperature (1d6 fire damage/round).
- **Assembly Lines**: Automated production of nonlethal armaments.
- **Testing Grounds**: Where new designs are... field-tested.

### Encounters (CR 3-6)
- **Forge Workers** (CR 1/2): Just trying to make a living.
- **Quality Inspectors** (CR 3): Ensuring compliance with standards.
- **Sabotage Teams** (CR 5): Attempting to disrupt production.

### Secrets
- **Flawed Prototype**: A weapon that fails "mercifully" by design.
- **Rebel Contacts**: Workers sympathetic to the resistance.

### Campaign Ties
- **Moral Choice**: Support nonlethal warfare or expose the hypocrisy?
- **Faction Play**: Sell to Nazarick or supply the resistance?
- **Consequences**: Ripple effects on future battles.

## 9. The Quiet Orchard  
*Sanctuary*  
**Region**: Hidden Valley  
**Faction Influence**: Independent (Sanctuary)

### Lore
A peaceful orchard where divination fails and names hold no power. A refuge for those who wish to disappear completely, if only for a little while.

### Features
- **Memory Trees**: Absorb and store the memories of those who sleep beneath them.
- **Trial Glades**: Areas that test visitors' intentions.
- **The Keeper**: An ancient treant who remembers everything... and forgets nothing.

### Encounters (CR 1-4)
- **Choir Sniffers** (CR 2): Tracking fugitives.
- **Syndicate Couriers** (CR 1): Delivering messages that don't exist.
- **Mysterious Benefactor** (CR ?): Leaves gifts for the worthy.

### Secrets
- **The Price of Peace**: The orchard's protection isn't free.
- **Hidden Grove**: Where the truly lost go to be forgotten.

### Campaign Ties
- **Downtime Haven**: Safe(ish) place to rest.
- **Future Crossroads**: Will this place survive the coming storm?
- **Character Arcs**: Personal quests may lead here.

## 10. The Echo Vault  
*Memory Vault*  
**Region**: Crystal Peaks  
**Faction Influence**: Nazarick (Research), Independent Scholars

### Lore
A cavern system where the memories of pre-Founding heroes are stored in crystal formations. To learn from the past, one must be willing to sacrifice pieces of themselves.

### Features
- **Memory Crystals**: Store experiences and skills.
- **Echo Guardians**: Test those who would claim the past's power.
- **The Well of Forgetting**: Where lost memories go... and sometimes return.

### Encounters (CR 4-6)
- **Memory Leeches** (CR 2): Feed on stray thoughts.
- **Echo Knights** (CR 5): Spectral warriors testing worth.
- **The Archivist** (CR 7): Keeper of the vault's secrets.

### Secrets
- **Ainz's Visit**: The Supreme Being left something behind.
- **The Silent Echo**: A memory that refuses to be remembered.

### Campaign Ties
- **Character Development**: Learn new abilities at a cost.
- **Lore Reveals**: Secrets of the pre-Founding era.
- **Personal Quests**: Face echoes of your own past.

## DM Notes: Using These Locations

### Scaling Encounters
- **Tier 1 (Levels 1-4)**: Focus on lower-CR enemies and social encounters.
- **Tier 2 (Levels 5-10)**: Add environmental hazards and faction complications.
- **Tier 3+ (Levels 11-20)**: Introduce planar echoes and mythic elements.

### Faction Influence
Track how the party's actions shift control of each location between:
- **Nazarick**: Order and control, but at a cost.
- **The Choir**: Purity and tradition, often to extremes.
- **The Syndicate**: Freedom and opportunity, with no guarantees.
- **Independent**: Balance and neutrality, if it can be maintained.

### Adventure Hooks
1. **The Salt Stair Secret**: The audit cache contains a prophecy about the party.
2. **Lyceum's Last Lesson**: The dean's echo knows what really caused the disaster.
3. **Roadside Assistance**: A refugee on the Crown Road recognizes Lyrin from "before."
4. **Market Day**: Broker Soot is selling information about the party's greatest weakness.
5. **Bell's Toll**: The Drowned Bell is ringing out of season, and the locals are nervous.
6. **Audit Trail**: The Sable Depot's records contain a clue to Nazarick's true plans.
7. **Off-Key**: The Farsung Menhir's song is changing, and not in a good way.
8. **Forged in Blood**: The Weeping Forge's latest prototype could turn the tide of the war.
9. **Orchard's End**: The Quiet Orchard's protection is failing, and the Keeper knows why.
10. **Echoes of Tomorrow**: The Echo Vault contains a memory of a future that must be prevented.

## 11. Kingdoms of History
*Ancient Empires*
**Region**: The Contested Lands (Central/Southern)
**Faction Influence**: Historical Legacy, Independent Warlords

### Lore
Two ancient rival kingdoms that defined the continent's history: The **Kingdom of Epstein** (wealthy, diplomatic) and the **Kingdom of Hate** (militaristic, legendary). Both are remnants of a bygone era dominated by the Warbound General Zachery Tokisaki.

### Features
- **Ruins of Iron**: The scarred battlefields of the "Era of Iron".
- **Isle of Pleasures**: The decadent capital of Epstein.
- **The Abyssal Barracks**: A massive, sealed high-level dungeon beneath the earth.
