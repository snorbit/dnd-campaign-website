# Detailed Encounters

This document provides in-depth descriptions for the encounters in the campaign, designed to provide the DM with all necessary tools to run dynamic and tactical scenes.

## Format Key
- **Hook**: The narrative trigger or initial setup.
- **Goal**: The primary objective for the party.
- **Map/Space**: Terrain details, dimensions, and atmospheric elements.
- **Features**: Interactive elements, hazards, and environmental advantages.
- **Enemies**: Combatants, their stats source, and behavior.
- **Timer**: Round-by-round events (Ticks) that alter the stakes.
- **Treasure**: Rewards, usually converting to favors or specific items.
- **XP**: Experience points for the encounter.

## Forest Encounters

### 1. Goblin Ambush (CR 1/4 - 1)
**Hook**: A wheel-broken cart blocks the forest road, its driver nowhere to be seen. A low groan comes from the bushes.
**Goal**: Survive the ambush and identify the attackers' affiliation.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: 20ft wide dirt road flanked by dense foliage.
- **Terrain**: Trees provide half cover; bushes are light obscurement.
- **Lighting**: Dappled sunlight (bright light).
**Features**:
- **Spiked Pit**: A DC 12 Perception check reveals a hidden pit trap (10ft deep, 1d6 falling + 1d6 piercing) in front of the cart.
- **Fallen Log**: Provides heavy cover for Small creatures (like Goblins).
**Enemies**:
- **4 Goblins** (MM p. 166). Two are hiding (+6 Stealth) with shortbows; two engage in melee.
- **Tactics**: The melee goblins use *Nimble Escape* to Disengage and lure PCs into the pit trap. Archers target spellcasters.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 3**: A horn blows; 2 more Goblins arrive from the rear if the fight isn't over.
**Treasure**: 2d6 gp, 1d4 silvered arrows, and a crude map to a nearby cave.
**XP**: 200

### 2. The Corrupted Grove (CR 3)
**Hook**: The forest grows unnervingly quiet. The scent of rotting flowers fills the air as the path opens into a dead glade.
**Goal**: Cleanse the grove by destroying the source of corruption or surviving the guardians.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Circular glade (60ft diameter) with a blackened ancient oak in the center.
- **Terrain**: Roots create difficult terrain throughout the glade.
- **Lighting**: Dim light (canopy is unnaturally thick).
**Features**:
- **Corrupting Spores**: At the start of each round, non-plants take 1 poison damage.
- **Grasping Roots**: DC 12 Str save to move more than 15ft in a turn.
**Enemies**:
- **1 Twig Blight**, **2 Vine Blights** (MM p. 32).
- **Tactics**: Vine Blights use *Constrict* to restrain targets within the spore cloud. The Twig Blight hides and attacks prone targets.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 4**: The ancient oak shudders, releasing a *Stinking Cloud* (DC 13 Constitution save) centered on itself.
**Treasure**: 1d4 *Goodberries*, a Druidic Focus made of petrified wood detailed with unholy runes.
**XP**: 700

### 3. Werewolf Pack (CR 5)
**Hook**: The full moon rises, and distant howling suddenly sounds—from three different directions, closing in fast.
**Goal**: Survive the night or drive off the pack leader.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Small clearing (30ft radius) surrounded by dense, tall pines.
- **Terrain**: Pine needles make stealth checks difficult (disadvantage) for non-rangers.
- **Lighting**: Bright moonlight (dim light in tree shadow).
**Features**:
- **Moonlight Vulnerability**: The werewolves regenerate normally, but silvered weapons gleam brightly here, granting advantage on intimidate checks brandishing them.
**Enemies**:
- **2 Werewolves** (MM p. 211) in hybrid form.
- **Tactics**: They use Pack Tactics to focus fire the weakest-looking party member. They attempt to bite to inflict lycanthropy.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 3**: Clouds obscure the moon. For 1 round, their AC drops by 1, but they gain advantage on Stealth.
**Treasure**: A silver dagger embedded in a tree (25 gp), 50 gp in scattered coins from previous victims.
**XP**: 1,800

## Mountain Encounters

### 4. Harpy Nest (CR 4)
**Hook**: A melodious, sorrowful song drifts down from the cliffs ahead, compelling the lead adventurer to step closer to the edge.
**Goal**: Cross the pass without falling; silence the harpies.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Narrow cliff path (5ft wide) with a 100ft drop on one side and sheer rock on the other.
- **Terrain**: Scree makes the path difficult terrain.
- **Lighting**: Bright sun, high altitude glare.
**Features**:
- **Echoing Walls**: Thunder damage deals double damage here but triggers a rockslide (DC 12 Dex save, 2d6 bludgeoning).
- **The Drop**: A fall is 10d6 damage.
**Enemies**:
- **2 Harpies** (MM p. 181).
- **Tactics**: Use *Luring Song* to draw PCs off the edge. They fly out of reach and attack with claws only when targets are charmed.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 2**: Strong updraft. Ranged attacks suffer disadvantage for 1 round; flying speed doubles.
**Treasure**: A nest containing 100 gp in coins and shiny trinkets (glass beads, mirrors).
**XP**: 1,100

### 5. Yeti Ambush (CR 6)
**Hook**: A sudden blizzard creates a "whiteout." Through the howling wind, massive silhouettes emerge from the snowdrifts.
**Goal**: Survive the cold and the beast.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Icy mountain saddle, 40ft wide, flanked by 15ft snowbanks.
- **Terrain**: Patches of slippery ice (DC 12 Dex save or fall prone if moving > half speed).
- **Lighting**: Heavy obscurement (Blizzard). Vision Limited to 15ft.
**Features**:
- **Extreme Cold**: DC 10 Con save every minute (or round 5) or take 1 level of exhaustion.
- **Snowbanks**: Can be burrowed into for total cover.
**Enemies**:
- **2 Yetis** (MM p. 305). *Note: Adjusted to 2 Yetis to match CR 6 challenge.*
- **Tactics**: One Yeti uses *Chilling Gaze* from stealth (Snow Camouflage), while the other rushes prone targets. They retreat if taken below 15 HP.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 4**: Looking Glass Ice. The blizzard clears momentarily, revealing the reflection of a larger predator (Abominable Yeti) in the ice, frightening the standard Yetis into a frenzy (advantage on attacks, disadvantage on AC).
**Treasure**: Frozen amulet (50 gp), Yeti fur (valuable to weavers).
**XP**: 2,300

## Urban Encounters

### 6. Thieves' Guild (CR 3)
**Hook**: A pickpocket bumps into a PC (Sleight of Hand +4 vs PP). Whether successful or not, they run into a dead-end alley where friends wait.
**Goal**: Recover stolen item or teach the guild a lesson.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: T-shaped alley, 10ft wide. Dead end is filled with crates.
- **Terrain**: Rooftops are 15ft high (Athletics DC 12 to climb).
- **Lighting**: Dim lantern light.
**Features**:
- **Clotheslines**: Can be cut (AC 10, HP 1) to drop wet laundry, blinding a target (blinded until end of turn).
- **Sewer Grate**: Small creatures can squeeze through to escape.
**Enemies**:
- **1 Spy** (Leader), **3 Thugs** (MM p. 349, 350).
- **Tactics**: Thugs use *Pack Tactics* to gang up. The Spy stays back, using Cunning Action to hide and snipe.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 3**: City Watch whistle blows. Enemies flee if not winning.
**Treasure**: 100 gp, thieves' tools, and a Guild insignia.
**XP**: 700

### 7. Doppelganger Infiltration (CR 5)
**Hook**: At a noble's estate, the party sees the exact double of a party member or a known NPC walking into a restricted room.
**Goal**: Expose the impostors without causing a diplomatic incident.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Lavish manor hallway with multiple doors and a grand staircase.
- **Terrain**: Polished marble floors (no effect, just fancy).
- **Lighting**: Bright crystal chandeliers.
**Features**:
- **Mirrors**: Large hallway mirrors grant advantage on Perception checks to spot ambushers behind.
- **Alarm Cord**: If pulled, summons 4 Guards (MM p. 347) in 1d4 rounds.
**Enemies**:
- **2 Doppelgangers** (MM p. 82).
- **Tactics**: They use *Read Thoughts* to gain advantage on attacks. If cornered, they shapeshift into a hostage or a guard to confuse the party.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 5**: The actual noble arrives. If the Doppelgangers are still active, they accuse the party of intrusion.
**Treasure**: 250 gp in valuables (silverware, jewelry), a letter of blackmail.
**XP**: 1,800

## Dungeon Encounters

### 8. Gelatinous Cube (CR 2)
**Hook**: The corridor ahead looks remarkably clean—polished, even.
**Goal**: Navigate the corridor without being digested.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: 10ft wide, 60ft long stone corridor.
- **Terrain**: Smooth stone.
- **Lighting**: Darkness.
**Features**:
- **Transparent Trap**: DC 15 Perception to spot the cube. If failed, the lead PC walks straight into it (Surprise).
- **Skeleton Debris**: Floating inside are bones and undigested metal gear.
**Enemies**:
- **1 Gelatinous Cube** (MM p. 242).
- **Tactics**: It takes up the entire width of the hall. It simply moves forward, engulfing everything.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 3**: The cube splits if it has taken at least 20 slashing damage, becoming two items (Gray Oozes stats effectively) or just reducing size to allow passage.
**Treasure**: Partially dissolved items (25 gp), a non-corroded magic ring (Ring of Swimming).
**XP**: 450

### 9. Mimic Colony (CR 4)
**Hook**: The party finds a vault room stacked with chests. Too many chests.
**Goal**: Retrieve the real treasure.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: 20x20ft room.
- **Terrain**: Cluttered. Difficult terrain due to "chests."
- **Lighting**: Torch light.
**Features**:
- **Sticky Floors**: Adhesive residue. DC 12 Str save to move if starting turn on a sticky tile.
- **False Hoard**: Only one chest is real.
**Enemies**:
- **2 Mimics** (MM p. 220).
- **Tactics**: They wait for interaction. Once revealed, they grapple and bite (Adhesive).
**Timer**:
- **Tick 10**: The door is also a Mimic (Door Mimic). It locks itself.
**Treasure**: 200 gp in coins inside the real chest, 1d4 potions of healing.
**XP**: 1,100

**Goal**: Survive the juggernaut.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Deep swamp, knee-deep water everywhere.
- **Terrain**: Difficult terrain.
- **Lighting**: Dark caused by dense canopy.
**Features**:
- **Lightning Storm**: A natural storm rages. Lightning strikes a random 5ft square each round (2d10 damage). If it hits the Mound, it heals.
**Enemies**:
- **1 Shambling Mound** (MM p. 270).
- **Tactics**: Engulfs the nearest target. It ignores difficult terrain.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 4**: The water level rises, requiring swim checks.
**Treasure**: Druidic focus (50 gp), a skeleton inside the mound wearing +1 Leather Armor.
**XP**: 1,800

## High-Level Encounters

### 30. Adult Black Dragon (CR 14)
**Hook**: The swamp water boils and turns black. A massive winged shape blocks out the sun.
**Goal**: Slay the tyrant of the swamp.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Sunken temple ruins, half-submerged.
- **Terrain**: Deep water (swim), crumbling pillars (cover).
- **Lighting**: Dim (swamp fog).
**Features**:
- **Lair Actions**: Insects swarm (Con save or difficult terrain), pools of water surge (knock prone).
- **Acidic Water**: Entering the water deals 1d6 acid damage.
**Enemies**:
- **1 Adult Black Dragon** (MM p. 88).
- **Tactics**: Uses *Frightful Presence* immediately. Flies between pillars to break line of sight. Uses Acid Breath on clustered groups.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 3**: The dragon dives, swimming stealthily to reposition for a surprise attack.
**Treasure**: 5,000 gp hoard, magical trident (+2), Potion of Water Breathing.
**XP**: 11,500

### 31. Lich's Phylactery Defense (CR 18)
**Hook**: A necrotic pulse knocks the party back. The Lich stands before a glowing soul-gem, laughing.
**Goal**: Destroy the phylactery and the Lich.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Circular arcane sanctum with floating platforms.
- **Terrain**: Void between platforms (fall into Astral Sea, effectively removed from combat for 1d4 rounds).
- **Lighting**: Pale green magical light.
**Features**:
- **Anti-Magic Zones**: Random 10ft spheres where magic doesn't work.
- **Soul Drain**: Lair action, tether to a PC, healing Lich for damage dealt.
**Enemies**:
- **1 Lich** (MM p. 202), **2 Death Knights** (MM p. 47).
- **Tactics**: Death Knights intercept melee attackers. Lich uses *Power Word Kill* on damaged foes and *Counterspell*.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 5**: The phylactery activates, restoring the Lich to full HP if not destroyed (AC 20, HP 100).
**Treasure**: Legendary magic items (Staff of Power), Phylactery shards (worth 10,000 gp to collectors).
**XP**: 22,500

**Hook**: The Dullahan maid adjusts her glasses. "You shall not pass without Lord Ainz's permission."
**Goal**: Defeat the Monk Commander.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Great Tomb Entrance Hall. Pristine marble.
- **Terrain**: Pillars provide cover.
- **Lighting**: Magical bright light.
**Features**:
- **Impact Shockwaves**: Her punches create 10ft thunderwaves (Str save DC 18 or prone).
**Enemies**: **Yuri Alpha** (Custom Monk/Dullahan stats).
**Tactics**: Focused melee. She stuns the healer, flips the tank, and intercepts projectiles.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 5**: She activates her "Fa han" (Head detaches to scout/attack from range).
**XP**: 20,000

### 81. Lupusregina Beta (CR 17)
**Hook**: A playful laugh echoes. "Are you ready to be eaten? You look delicious!"
**Goal**: Survive the hunt.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Carne Village Ruins (Fake).
- **Terrain**: Burning buildings.
- **Lighting**: Firelight.
**Enemies**: **Lupusregina Beta** (Werewolf Cleric). **4 Dire Wolves**.
**Tactics**: She casts *Flame Strike* while invisible. She relishes causing pain (targets unarmored PCs). Transforms into Hybrid form at 50% HP.
**XP**: 18,000

### 82. Narberal Gamma (CR 16)
**Hook**: "Insects." Lightning crackles around the maid as `Fly` activates.
**Goal**: Ground the caster.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Open Sky / Rooftops.
- **Terrain**: No cover.
- **Lighting**: Stormy.
**Enemies**: **Narberal Gamma** (Doppelganger Wizard).
**Tactics**: *Chain Lightning* opener. *Greater Invisibility*. She stays at max range (120ft+).
**Timer**:
- **Tick 3**: Casts *Twin Maximize Magic* (Metamagic).
**XP**: 15,000

### 83. CZ2128 Delta (CR 15)
**Hook**: A red laser dot appears on your chest. "Target acquired."
**Goal**: Close the distance before you are sniped.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Maze-like corridors.
- **Terrain**: Trapped tiles (Explosive Runes).
- **Lighting**: Dim.
**Enemies**: **CZ2128 Delta** (Automaton Artificer/Ranger).
**Tactics**: Snipes from 300ft. Uses holographic decoys. If approaching, she uses repulsor blasts.
**XP**: 13,000

### 84. Solution Epsilon (CR 14)
**Hook**: The beautiful maid smiles, but her skin ripples. The floor melts into acid.
**Goal**: Don't get absorbed.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Dining Hall.
- **Terrain**: Acid pools (10ft radius, 4d6 acid).
- **Lighting**: Candlelight.
**Enemies**: **Solution Epsilon** (Slime Assassin).
**Tactics**: Grapples and Engulfs (swallows whole). Acid damage destroys armor (-1 AC/round).
**XP**: 11,500

### 85. Entoma/Ricole (CR 13)
**Hook**: The maid's voice is two overlapping sounds—a cute girl and a clicking insect.
**Goal**: Burn away the swarm.
**Map/Space**:
- **Layout**: Web-choked forest.
- **Terrain**: Webs (Restrained).
- **Lighting**: Dark.
**Enemies**: **Entoma Vasilissa Zeta** (Entomancer).
**Tactics**: Summons Swarms of Insects (Meat Shields). Uses *Web* and *Insect Plague*.
**Timer**:
- **Tick 4**: She eats a "snack" (healing potion/limb) to regenerate.
**XP**: 10,000
